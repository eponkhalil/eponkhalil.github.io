About

I am an economist with an MSc in Financial Economics from the University of Glasgow and a BSc in Economics from North South University, Dhaka. My work sits at the intersection of development economics, digital policy, and causal inference.

I am currently seeking a funded PhD position to investigate how digital economic policies — including broadband access and digital literacy programmes — shape economic outcomes, using both traditional econometric methods and emerging causal machine learning approaches.

Alongside my research pursuits, I work as Financial Management Officer at The Whithorn Trust, a UK-based NGO, where I manage financial operations remotely from Dhaka, Bangladesh.

Research Interests

  • Causal inference and causal AI in economics
  • Digital inclusion and broadband policy
  • Development economics and policy evaluation
  • Heterogeneous treatment effects using econometric and ML methods
